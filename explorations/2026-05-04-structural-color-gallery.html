Structural Color.

↳ A gallery of mappings between visible light (380–740 nm) and physical structure. Color here is not pigment but interference, diffraction, and lattice: the way a peacock feather, a beetle's elytron, an opal, and a soap bubble all conjure hue from architecture rather than absorption.

Helium·I

Emission Catalogue

Eight visible-light emission lines of neutral helium (He I). Each line is a real photon: the colour you see is the colour the atom emitted. The concentric mandala diagrams that photon's origin — outer rim shows the line's exact emitted hue; the photon belt radiates wavefronts outward, ripple frequency tracking λ; the upper orbital ring is the excited state the electron fell from; the lower orbital ring is where it came to rest; the centre carries the wavelength itself. Angular lobes encode L, radial bands encode n − L. A single dot marks parahelium (S = 0); three dots mark orthohelium (S = 1). Geometry is schematic; wavelengths and term symbols are NIST-ASD ground truth.

↳ Suite 06 / The Axiom Stack

Assumptions·Axioms

Every Visualization Is a Choice

The galleries above made choices. Some are physics — Schrödinger's equation, the Bohr formula, the photon. Some are conventions — eV vs cm⁻¹, vacuum vs air wavelength, LS vs jj coupling. Some are perceptual — Bruton 1996 vs CIE 1931. Some are aesthetic — log vs linear, polar vs Cartesian, particle clouds vs sharp rings. Each choice is a design primitive that rests on the choice below it. This suite pulls each layer out of the stack so you can see what depends on what. The data — NIST-ASD ground truth — is the same. The eigenvalues, the units, the colour model, and the geometry are what move.

↳ Suite 07 / The Normalizer

Ritz·Combination

Color Is Decorative · Ratios Are Structural

The previous suites tied colour to wavelength. But colour is what humans see — visibility is a 360 nm slice of a spectrum that runs from radio to gamma. The structure of an emission spectrum lives in the ratios between lines, and those ratios live across the complete spectrum, not inside the visible window. This suite separates the two: lay the data on a complete-spectrum axis (Lyman α in the EUV through 21 cm hydrogen hyperfine), look for an equation that normalizes the ratios across all atoms, and let colour come along only as a label for the visible slice. The equation is the Ritz Combination Principle — every observed wavenumber is a difference of two atomic terms — and its Rydberg+quantum-defect generalization for elements heavier than hydrogen.

ν̃ = 1/λ wavenumber (cm⁻¹)
T_n = R/n² · hydrogen term value
n* = n − δ effective principal quantum number
ν̃ = T_l − T_u Ritz Combination
R = 109 737 cm⁻¹ Rydberg
colour = label, not axis
↳ Suite 08 / Phase Space

Multi-Axis·Atlas

The Full EM Spectrum · All Sequences · 2-D Slices of N-D Space

Correction logged: the complete spectrum is the electromagnetic spectrum — gamma-ray through radio, ≥ 20 decades in λ. Atomic emission is one slice; molecular vibration, X-ray fluorescence, hyperfine splittings, plasma masers, and CMB radiation are others. Every observed line carries a row in a relational schema (NIST-ASD canonically; HITRAN, Splatalogue, CHIANTI as siblings) with ~25 measurable quantities attached: wavelength variants, level energies, statistical weights, Einstein A_ki, oscillator strength gf, lifetimes, line-broadening parameters, isotope shifts, Zeeman g-factors. The spectroscopic state space is high-dimensional; every visualization above (and every visualization possible) is a 2-D projection. This suite makes the projection explicit, names the axes, and walks the same data through several different planes of the space.

Colour-rule audit (revised): Bruton(λ) applies only inside the visible band 380–740 nm. Outside the visible band, line ticks render achromatic (a single neutral white). The earlier "decorative" purples-for-UV / reds-for-IR were arbitrary and have been retired across Suites 06, 07, and 08. Background region shading is now gray chrome; only the visible band is highlighted, marking "Bruton applies here." PS·05 (Grotrian) and PS·06 (Six Projections) are also expanded to include the He I 1083 nm NIR line — they are no longer visible-band-only.
